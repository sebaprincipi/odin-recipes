Milanesa

Description

Milanesa” refers to a thin cut of meat that is breaded and pan-fried, and then served with fried potatoes, a salad, and sometimes rice and beans. A typical Argentine dish, here it is a favorite with children and adults alike.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ pound of thin cut top round beef steaks 6 steaks
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 cups of bread crumbs or saltine crackers ground into crumbs see note
  • 2 garlic cloves chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • ½ teaspoon black peppercorns
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • 1Salt to taste

Steps

  1. i>Grind the garlic clove and peppercorn in a mortar.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk the two eggs lightly and add the garlic and peppercorn mixture. I usually add a Tbsp. of water to the mortar after grinding the garlic and peppercorns.
  3. In a large dish, spread the bread crumbs mixed with salt, and have another dish ready to place the steaks after breading.
  4. Dip the steaks into the egg mixture.
  5. Using kitchen tongs, place one steak into the crumbs, turn to coat both sides. Patting on lightly to make sure the coating adheres to the steak. Place the already-breaded steak aside on a plate. Repeat this step with the remaining steaks.
  6. Once you finish breading all the steaks you will proceed to fry them. Using a large skillet, heat ½ inch of oil over medium-high heat. Make sure the oil is hot before placing the steaks.
